Models' performance across different session lengths (MRR@20)

We assess the influence of varying session lengths (short, medium and long) on the two most effective sequential and GNN models by using DIGI and RETAIL datasets. Table presents the results for MRR@20. The categorization of sessions into short, medium, and long follows the guidelines discussed in a recent survey article [1]: short sessions contain ≤ 3 items, medium sessions contain more than 3 but ≤ 10 items, and long sessions consist of more than 10 items. Each category has its own challenges, see [1].
For the DIGI dataset, GRU4Rec achieves the best results across all session categories, while its variant NARM ranks second. Among GNN models, GNRRW achieves the first position on short sessions, while, SR-GNN significantly outperforms GNRRW for the other two categories. For the RETAIL dataset, GRU4Rec outperforms all other models with a clear margin for short sessions. While, SR-GNN obtains the highest rank for medium and longer sessions. Notably, GRU4Rec and SR-GNN are pioneering models designed for session-based tasks. These models are still competitive and outperforming other complex models across all session categories.
